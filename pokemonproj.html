Project Overview

For this project I chose a dataset of Pokémon statistics such as type, height, speed, etc., and used Pandas to analyze the data and create data visualizations. I chose to investigate the question: Is the Pokémon's weight a determining factor for it's catch rate? Below is a brief overview of the code and scatterplot used in this project.

Data Visualization Example

After importing the dataset from Kaggle, cleaning it, and determining my inquiry question, I made visualizations of the data. Below is the code used for making a scatterplot of the Pokémon's weight vs catch rate. I also added the hue of the datapoints to be of the Pokémon's base HP (Health Points).

Key:

  • 255 = 33.3% catch rate at full HP (with a regular Pokéball)
  • 3 = 0.4% catch rate at full HP (with a regular Pokéball)

Code:

In [ ]:
sns.scatterplot(x='Weight', y='Catch rate', hue="HP", data=df)
            plt.title("Weight vs Catch rate")
Out[ ]:
Text(0.5, 1.0, 'Weight vs Catch rate')

The data shows that there are more Pokémon with a catch rate of 255 when their weight is lighter than 200. There's only one outlier with a catch rate of 255 with a weight of 1000. Where we see the strongest relationship between the weight and the catch rate is in Pokémon with weights lighter than 200.

Final Result

Looking at the data visualizations, weight seems to be one of the determining factors, but it's not the only factor for it's catch rate. To see my full data analysis and conclusion check out the links at the top of this page for the Colab notebook!
