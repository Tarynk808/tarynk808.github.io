Question: Is weight a deciding factor of the catch rate?

With the available data, weight does seem to be one of the deciding factors of it's catch rate. There are more Pokmon with a catch rate of 255 when their weight is lighter than 200. There's only one outlier with a catch rate of 255 with a weight of 1000. Where we see the strongest relationship between the weight and the catch rate is in Pokmon with weights lighter than 200. I noticed that most of the Pokmon with a ctach rate of 3 were Legendary or Mythical Pokmon. Also, the Pokmon with a catch rate of 255 were generally basic Pokmon which does make sense.

With my visualizations I choose to also look at the base HP of the Pokmon because usually heavier Pokmon will have more base HP, and vise versa. However, I was surprised to see that there was a variety of Pokmon in each HP category with varying catch rates. For example, there are Pokmon with 50 base HP that have a low catch rate and ones that have a high catch rate.

A limitation of my data is that there aren't as many Pokmon with weights of 400+ so the data could be skewed a little bit because there is less data points. For example, weight might not be that big of factor for it's catch rate, but because there aren't as many Pokmon with heavier weights, our data could be inaccurate. For furthur investigations, I would suggest to look at the Pokmon species because some Pokmon have more skittish natures that I think can affect their catch rate, so it would be good to see if that is a factor. However, to get a full analysis I would suggest looking at all of the Pokmon statistics such as abilities and where they spawn because I know Pokmon that spawn on the first routes usually have higher catch rates.
